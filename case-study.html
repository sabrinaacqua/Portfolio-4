Case Study 1

Non-Profit Redesign

SeaBlue Canada Website Redesign

Executive Summary

I worked on a group project to redesign a non-profit organization's website with the aim of improving its usability. As part of this project, we were responsible for creating a simple, interactive website prototype, as well as a corresponding mobile prototype.

Project Overview

We redesigned the Sea Blue Canada website to make it more visually appealing and easy to use. We want to make interactions easier for existing users and find ways to attract more users as well.

Role & Responsibility

Group consisting of four people, one of them being me. We all collaborated together for 95% of the project. The last 5% was towards the end of the project, half of the group iterated the prototypes and the other half worked on the presentation.

Tools Used

Figma, FigJam, Trello, Paper & Pen, Google Drive, Canva

Timeline & Scope

3-week period to complete project

User Research

In the first phase of our project, we started off by creating a proto-persona, to get the idea of who our user might be. Once completing this, we drafted an interview plan. Surveys and user interviews, 18-40 year olds intrested in ocean conservation, were conducted to evaluate the usabiltiy of the current site and to identify SeaBlue's target audience, so we could understand the demographic and possible motivations. We found that users expperienced couldn't understand what SeaBlue was and had issues navigating the platform. We then we used the data from our affinity diagram to contruct a user persona and empathy map. We found it resembled our proto-persona but helped us more clearly understand our user.

Definiton & Synthesis

When it came to the definition phase, we finalized our user insight statement as well as our hypothesis and problem. User Insight: Environmentally conscious young adults like Emilia are deeply concerned about the state of the oceans, but feel helpless to take on the governments and massive industries in control of oceans resources. Hypothesis: We suspect users visiting the SeaBlue website might leave feeling unclear on who SeaBlue is and what they do. Users may care deeply about the cause but struggle to navigate the site and learn how they can take action or become involved. Problem: Environmentally conscious individuals who care about ocean preservation are doing their best to minimize their carbon footprint; but when exploring the SeaBlue Canada website they feel overwhelmed with information and confused with information and confused how to take action.
